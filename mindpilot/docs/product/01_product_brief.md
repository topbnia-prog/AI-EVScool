# Product Brief

## Core Thesis

MindPilot turns an AI user from a passenger into a pilot.

Most children ask AI for answers. MindPilot teaches them to command the tool, check it, challenge it, and stay mentally independent.

## Audience

- Buyer: parents or guardians of children ages 10-16
- User: child operator
- First market: Israel
- MVP content language: Hebrew

## Parent Pain

Parents see children using AI without understanding it. The child may copy answers, trust hallucinations, or become dependent on the tool. School does not yet teach practical AI independence at the level families need.

## Child Pain

The child usually does not describe the pain directly. The real problem is that they ask AI for answers, receive unreliable output, and do not know how to check, steer, or improve it.

## Parent Offer

Give the child a long-term advantage: critical thinking, AI verification, strong prompting, and independent reasoning.

## Child Offer

You are not a user. You are an operator. You learn to control a powerful system.

## What MindPilot Is Not

- Not a generic AI course
- Not homework automation
- Not a chatbot that gives answers
- Not a school replacement
- Not a public leaderboard or competition

## Product Principles

- Questions over answers
- Verification over trust
- Effort over talent
- Missions over lessons
- Metrics over grades
- Personal progress over public comparison
- Safety over speed
