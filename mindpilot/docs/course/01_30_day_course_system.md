# 30-Day Course System

## Course Shape

The first public course is a 30-day mission path. It is based on early testing methodology, but contains no personal child data and no private case details.

## Age Branches

MindPilot uses two branches:

- Junior: ages 10-13
- Senior: ages 14-16

The learning goal is the same. The branch changes:

- language complexity
- text length
- examples
- challenge openness
- mentor tone

Most missions exist for both branches. Content is stored as prepared educational material. The AI mentor adapts delivery and dialogue, but does not generate the course from scratch.

## Weekly Structure

Week 1: What AI really is
- AI as tool, not mind
- Prediction, not truth
- Hallucinations
- Knowledge cutoff
- Confidence is not reliability

Week 2: How to command AI
- Prompt structure
- Context, goal, constraints, format
- Decomposition
- Better follow-up questions
- Asking AI to expose uncertainty without requesting hidden chain-of-thought

Week 3: How to verify AI
- Show me evidence
- Fact vs opinion
- Compare sources
- Compare two AI answers
- Detect unsupported claims

Week 4: AI as tool, not replacement
- What to delegate and what not to delegate
- Homework and cheating boundaries
- Personal data boundaries
- Building with AI
- Final operator reflection

Day 30:
- Final operator file written in the child's own words
- Parent-facing completion summary
- Recommendation for next course

## Daily Limits

- One completed meaningful action is enough for daily activity.
- Maximum two missions per day.
- Third mission is blocked by backend code, not only by prompt.
- The second mission of a day opens only after the first mission reflection is completed.

## Unlock Rules

- Missions unlock sequentially.
- Reflection must be answered before the next mission opens.
- The AI mentor may guide reflection, but may not answer for the child.

## Free Tier

The first two missions must be among the strongest content in the product. They should create the first "operator" feeling before the paywall.
